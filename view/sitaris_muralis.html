IUCN version 2001
Criteria description D2
Comments A difficult species to detect due to the likelihood of urban sites being hidden from general view & often on private land with no public access, plus the short lifespan and relative inactivity of adults, which apparently seldom move far from the host bee nest (Brock, 2010). IUCN criteria satisfied are based on the very small or restricted population: geographic range restricted in area of occupancy (less than 1 tetrad) with severely fragmented populations (only two reports in past 25 years). No sub-fossil remains have yet been found in Britain (Buckland & Buckland, 2006), but current understanding suggests a long-term native.
