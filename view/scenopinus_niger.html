designation description Nationally Rare - Occurring in 15 or fewer hectads in Great Britain. Includes rare species qualifying under the main IUCN criteria.
IUCN version NA
Criteria description NA
Comments Recorded from 13 hectads since 1990 from Wales to East Anglia and to the south English coast. Older records extend to the West Country, and there are single records from Cumbria and Highland. There is no obvious decline but there is an moderately high proportion (18%) of old and recent hectads in common, which suggests that populations may be localised in ancient woodland and parklands. Although uncommon, the fly's secretive behaviour is likely to have led to under-recording. Moved beyond NR under Criterion 2.
