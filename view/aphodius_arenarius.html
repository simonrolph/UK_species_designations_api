Comments Since 1990, only known with certainty from the Carmarthenshire coast, Wales, from The Brecks in Norfolk and from East Kent (including the Kent Downs) â€“ seven sites in total and five hectads. Possibly under recorded. There are plausible threats but little if any indication of continuing decline. Between the two main recording periods, there has been a reduction of some 77% AoO in terms of hectads occupied. However, the more recent recording period has seen no change in the hectad count (unless potentially unreliable records are considered which would again suggest a decline from 7 to 3 hectads). One of the five post-2000 sites at Brandon may face future development and has possibly been altered recently, representing a potential 20% loss of the currently extant regional population. Conversely, the species is not under threat at Weeting Heath NR, Norfolk, where sympathetic management is in place, nor at Cranwich Camp, Norfolk where grazing and topsoil scraping have recently been established. The dune system population in Carmarthenshire may be threatened by coastal erosion or vegetative succession. Due to the possibility of under recording, an AoO which currently encompasses 6 or more modern locations and no definite proof of continuing decline, despite the presence of plausible threat, a non-IUCN designation of near-Threatened is advised. Hectad counts in parenthesis in this spreadsheet indicate maxima if uncertain dates and potentially unreliable records are included. The final analysis has used only non-parenthesis counts.
