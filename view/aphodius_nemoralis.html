designation description A taxon is Data Deficient when there is inadequate information to make a direct, or indirect, assessment of its risk of extinction based on its distribution and/or population status. A taxon in this category may be well studied, and its biology well known, but appropriate data on abundance and/or distribution are lacking. Criteria description NA
Comments This is a difficult species to locate because its typical habitat of grazed upland and montane woodland may be difficult to access and woodland Aphodiines are generally more difficult to find than those in open habitats (pers obs.). Its main area of distribution (Scotland and northern England) is also poorly recorded, so although there are only nine post-1990 hectads for the species, we can conclude that it is likely to be more widespread than this paucity of records might indicate. There is no obvious ongoing decline. Its GB Rarity Status of Nationally Scarce 'NS' reflects a cautious approach. Future survey should target this species with a view to evaluating its true status. Currently, it is Data Deficient.
