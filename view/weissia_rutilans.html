Source description Northern Ireland Priority Species List - March 2010. A list of threatened species requiring conservation action in Northern Ireland. Selection criteria- 1. Listed as a UK Priority Species. 2. Rapid decline (2% per year). 3. Decline (1% year) with Northern Ireland being a stronghold (S) consisting of either >50% Irish population or >20% UK population/range or with the Irish or UK population restricted (R) to Northern Ireland or with the Irish or UK population restricted (R) to Northern Ireland i.e. Decline + S or R. 4. Rare (confined to a small population of one or two sites in Northern Ireland) with Northern Ireland being a stronghold (S) consisting of either >50% Irish population or >20% UK population/range or with the Irish or UK population restricted (R) to Northern Ireland i.e. Rare + S or R. 5. At least 20% of international population of species or well-recognised subspecies occurring in Northern Ireland. 6. Irish Red Data Book (RDB) species classed as critically endangered (CR), endangered (EN) or vulnerable (VU). 7. Red-listed species in either Birds of Conservation Concern in Ireland (BOCCI) or the UK Birds of Conservation Concern (UK BOCC) lists.
