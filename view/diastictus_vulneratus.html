Criteria description D2
Comments Despite recent pitfall trapping programmes at both Weeting Heath and Stanford, the species has not been rediscovered at these sites. At Cranwich, the site where it has most recently been found and where it has been found most frequently, it is actually very difficult to find (pers obs.) and is evidently at low population levels and vulnerable to changes in the nature of the grassland. Grazing or mechanical scarification is required at Breckland sites to create a sand and short turf mosaic rather than allowing site deterioration to succession to rank grassland. Succession and a change in sward height from short to tall would effect an increase in soil RH and a temperature drop, neither of which suit this thermophilic beetle. Recent decline is suspected but not proven. On the basis of there being fewer than 5 modern locations and plausible threats in the form of habitat deterioration at at least two of its current sites, a designation of VU D2 is advised.
