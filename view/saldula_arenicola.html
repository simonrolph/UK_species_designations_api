IUCN version 2001
Criteria description NA
Comments Recent records show S. arenicola to be locally frequent over a sizeable area of SE England north to Huntingdonshire (P. Kirby pers. comm.) where it is associated with sand and gravel pits. The colonisation of these areas and habitats is a fairly recent phenomenon; the species has traditionally been confined to south the coast between Hampshire and Devon (where it still occurs). Kirby suggests the 'new' (inland) and 'old' (coastal) S. arenicola may be different in some way and should be assessed separately - although at present the information required to justify this is not available.
