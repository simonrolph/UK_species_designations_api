Critically Endangered - The risk of extinction for birds in Great Britain Sep 2017 British Birds 110: 502-517.

Designated name Rissa tridactyla
Common name Kittiwake
Source The risk of extinction for birds in Great Britain Sep 2017 British Birds 110: 502-517.
Source description The first formal Red List Category for birds based on IUCN criteria and guidelines. This assessment (No 34 in the Species Status series) is not an alternative to BoCC and does not replace or supersede it. It is recommended to use the BoCC status as the starting point for identifying which birds are most deserving of conservation action. This assessment focusses on regular breeding and over-wintering birds of Great Britain, thus excluding birds in the Isle of Man, Channel Islands and northern Ireland. It includes 289 species and 68 subspecies on the British list and excludes non-natives, vagrants and scarce migrants. For species that have substantial and at least partially distinct breeding and non-breeding populations in GB (defined as having a population in the non-breeding season that is more than twice the size of the breeding population), notably waterbirds, the breeding and wintering populations are assessed separately. Therefore a species is assigned an IUCN Red List category for its breeding and wintering populations. Some notable bird subspecies, all of those for which Britain is of international importance, i.e. more than 20% of the northwest European flyway (wildfowl), East Atlantic flyway (waders) or European (all others) population were also assessed. These are national assessments determining the status of birds in Great Britain, however influences at regional level have also been considered if there is significant immigration of individuals from outside GB likely to breed which could rescue populations from extinction. For non-breeding taxa the assessment considers if conditions both inside and outside the region are deteriorating. Subsequently 22 species have been downgraded to consider this contribution.
URL source https://britishbirds.co.uk/article/risk-extinction-birds-great-britain/
Date designated 2017-09-01
Reporting category Red listing based on 2001 IUCN guidelines
Designation Critically Endangered
Designation abbreviation Bird_RedList_GB_post2001-CR_Breeding
designation description A taxon is Critically Endangered when it is facing an extremely high risk of extinction in the wild in the immediate future, as defined by any of the criteria A to E.
IUCN version 2001
Criteria description A4a
Comments The status of breeding Kittiwake was assessed as being Critically Endangered in Great Britain under criterion A4a, due to an observed, estimated, inferred, projected or suspected population decline, where the time period includes both the past and future (1985 to 2024), of at least 80% (81%) over 3 generations (39 years) using a Lowess log smoothing.
