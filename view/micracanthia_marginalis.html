Designated name Micracanthia marginalis
Common name NA
Source A review of the Hemiptera of Great Britain: The Aquatic and Semi-aquatic Bugs
Source description This report was commissioned to update the national status of aquatic and semi-aquatic bugs using IUCN methodology for assessing threat. It covers all species of aquatic and semi-aquatic bugs (Heteroptera) in Great Britain, identifying those that are rare and/or under threat as well as non-threatened and non-native species. A.A. Cook November 2015.
URL source http://publications.naturalengland.org.uk/publication/5132432324952064
Date designated 2015-11-20
Reporting category Red listing based on 2001 IUCN guidelines
Designation Vulnerable
Designation abbreviation RedList_GB_post2001-VU
designation description A taxon is Vulnerable when it is not Critically Endangered or Endangered but is facing a high risk of extinction in the wild in the medium term future.
IUCN version 2001
Criteria description D2
Comments Two post 1989 records on NBN Gateway for Cumbria and Kent. The Kent record is incorrect (confirmed by Tony Witts, Kent and Medway Biological Records Centre). The Cumbrian record was for for Solway Mosses (S. Hewitt in 1991 & 1993). There are two post 1989 records for Surrey (Denton 2007). Southwood & Leston (1959) considered M. marginalis to be a shrinking species, now surviving precariously'. P. Kirby's comment: 'I've not seen it for years, and wonder whether a higher status might be justified, in view of its propensity to form small colonies and consequent sensitivity to habitat change'. The apparent decline in AOO in the period 1990 - 2013 may in part be due to additional records for the previous period obtained during Welsh Peatland Invertebrate Survey, but even if the species is still present in the few (3) Welsh sites the known AOO would still be small. The most northerly known site before 1991 was Thorne Moors in South Yorkshire where M. marginalis was recorded in 1980, but it hasn't been recorded there since, despite an extensive survey of the site in the 1990's (B. Eversham pers.comm.). The status of VU rather than EN is appropriate, despite the small known AOO (<500 km2) and sensitive heathland habitat, in view of probable under-recording.
Reporting category sort order Fc
