Comments Originally recorded by Wood (1886) from seed potatoes, these and the underside of logs are the most often recorded situations for this species. Owen proposed that this species might in fact be an established synanthropic alien, since it has almost exclusively been recorded from artificial habitats - also only urban sites. Widespread in south of England (Denton, 1996); from Kent to Cornwall (Owen, 1999). May be more widespread than current records suggest due to its subterranean habits (Denton, 1997). May well be spreading (Mann & Angus, 2002).
